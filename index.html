The issue


Climate change is a global issue that can have drastic effects on earths future in fact, many issues that are appearing now have been attributed to climate change

Cause

Greenhouse Effect
CO₂ levels increased from the pre-industrial 280 ppm to our current-day 402.56 ppm.

Symptoms

Rising Sea Levels
Global Sea leves rose 178 milimeters in the last century.
Warming Oceans
The ocean's temperature showed an increase of 0.2℃ since 1969.
Rising Average Temperature
The earth's average temperatures showed an increase of 0.8℃ since 1880.
Longer seasons
Spring has increased by 3 weeks in the past 50 years in britan.
Melting Ice
The Greenland ice sheets lost 150 to 250 km² of mass per year between 2002 and 2006 while the Antarctic ice sheets lost 152 km² per year.
Ocean acidification
Since the beginning of the industrial revolution, acidity of ocean water increased almost 30%.
Stronger Natural Disasters
By 2080, hurricanes could be half a step up on our current scale of destructive power.

The united states and canada are the largest producers of greenhouse gasses.

Energy consumption

Coal and fossil-fuel power plants are the largest producers of greenhouse gasses, by using less power, we can reduce the pollution caused by these power plants.
Examples at home
- Replace your lightbulbs for the eco-friendly cfl bulbs.
- Install solar panels to produce some of your own power.
Examples at school
- Make sure that you turn off monitors when you walk away from them.
- Turn off lights when you are the last one exiting a room.

Waste

By reducing the amount of waste that is produced, it helps you conserve energy, and reduce pollution caused by waste disposal services.
Examples at home
- Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.
- Compost your excess food in your yard if your community does not accept green bins.
Examples at school
- Reduce, Reuse, Recycle.
- Ensure that you are not wasting any school resources.
